Adjectives


Overview of Grammar topic:

An adjective is a word that describes or defines a noun or a pronoun that acts as the subject of a sentence.

Adjectives of quality are those adjectives that describe the quality of a noun. They give us information on the state, condition or character of the noun.

Adjectives of quantity are adjectives that modify a noun by indicating any number or any quantity of countable or uncountable nouns.

The three forms of adjectives are:

  • The Positive or Absolute Form
  • The Comparative Form
  • The Superlative Form

Important note:

Form Usage Example
Positive or Absolute Form Used to describe a single noun without any comparison. This is a tall building.
Comparative Form Used to compare two nouns or pronouns. This building is taller than that one.
Superlative Form Used to compare three or more nouns or pronouns, or to show the highest degree of a quality. This is the tallest building in the city.
